LogLM vs. ML

Comparing Deep Learning-Powered Tempo LogLM with Traditional ML/UEBA Solutions

Traditional machine-learning approaches in cybersecurity often rely on static and rule-based methods that can become inefficient when threats evolve. In contrast, DeepTempo’s Tempo LogLM employs a transformer-based deep learning model pretrained on vast amounts of log data, resulting in superior accuracy, rapid adaptability, and transparent insights.

Criteria for Model Evaluation

  • Accuracy: Measures the precision and recall of anomaly detection. A higher rating means fewer false positives and negatives.
  • Adaptability: Reflects how quickly the solution can deliver value and adapt to new threats or domains.
  • Extensibility: Assesses the solution’s ability to integrate with various SIEM systems and broader security ecosystems.
  • Scalability: Evaluates how well the solution manages large volumes of log data and grows with your organization.
  • Explainability: Considers how clearly the solution’s outputs can be interpreted, helping analysts understand the reasons behind alerts.
  • Ease of Deployment: Looks at the simplicity of integrating the solution into existing environments.
  • Operations Cost: Measures the efficiency in terms of log ingestion, processing, and storage costs.
  • Visibility: Evaluates the breadth of log sources and types the solution can process.

Model Criteria

While Accuracy is crucial – both low false positives and false negatives are essential for reducing the burden on your security team and ensuring effective protection – so too are Adaptability and Explainability.

  • Accuracy: Both low false positives and false negatives are crucial. Low false positives reduce the burden on your security team, while low false negatives indicate the model's effectiveness in protecting your organization.
  • Adaptability: Foundation models like LogLM quickly transfer knowledge from prior environments to new ones, reducing the time to value and lessening the operational burden of repeated retraining.
  • Explainability: To be truly useful, LogLMs must provide clear context—such as impacted entities and correlations with MITRE ATT&CK patterns—so that security teams can act on alerts.
